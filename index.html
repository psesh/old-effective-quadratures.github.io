Digital twins made here.

Digital twins are virtual representations of a physical asset, ranging from aircrafts and rockets, to bridges and buildings, to factories and entire operational environments. Twins are asset-specific and like their physical counterpart they are always evolving. This evolution is typically tracked by a wide-array of sensors and physics-based knowledge. Digital twins can vary from emulating design and manufacturing to operations, and are motivated by improved asset-specific performance, predictive maintenance and physical analysis. Effective Quadratures provides a unique platform to build and deploy digital twins, fusing in sensor data with physics-based knowledge in a holistic and principled way.


Sensor fusion
Pipelining computer vision and machine learning models for extracting data from heterogenous sensors, ranging from flow sensors to images and videos.

Physics-driven understanding
Bespoke wrappers for interrogating and evaluating a range of physics-based models, including fluid dynamics, structural mechanics, computer-aided design.

Decision making
Utilising our equadratures package that uses sensor and physics-based data to arrive at decisions. It is the platform for developing and deploying digital twins.
